How we met

Adriana and Samir met the new fashioned way... yes, you thought right! ... they met through an online dating app! :D They began chatting less than a month into Samir having moved to California (the Universe was trying to make it happen!). Adriana can remember that one of their first conversations included popping bubble wrap, because Samir had just moved into his new place and had a ton of furniture to unpack.

Not long after, Adriana asked Samir out to dinner while she was on a flight back from Boston visting friends, and Samir unequivocally agreed. Because Adriana still lived at home, they decided to have dinner somewhere closer to where Samir lived in Japantown San Jose (plus there were better restuarents in San Jose anyway). The first date started with Adriana frantically looking for parking, while Samir watched her circle around and around the parking lot a total of 3 times before Adriana found a spot. They were meeting at a sushi restaurant, because what better place to have dinner than at a sushi place in Japantown. Samir laughed when he saw how frustrated Adriana was getting.

Dinner soon turned into dessert at a ice cream mochi shop Samir reallyed liked, and there they talked aboout llamas, troll fest, and many other things. That date in Japantown was the start of a great adventure. Fast forward to present and Adriana and Samir have enjoyed travelling across the globe from mountains, to cities, to beaches and back. Adriana and Samir have had a wonderful 6 years together and are looking forward to many many more.

Events

27th November

Mehndi 1PM - 5PM

It's one of the oldest traditional Indian pre wedding ceremony where the bride and groom attend the event together and a professional mehndi artist applies mehndi to the bride's hands and feet. This will be having a celebratory festival feel to it with people wearing vibrant clothes and dancing, singing, playing games and a lot more.

Cocktail Night 7PM - 12AM

This event is no way close to ethnic. The bride & groom would like to have some "shots before the knot". With booze flowing down the floor and the western attire on, everyone would be busy showing off their new moves on the stage which they have finely honed. Lastly, expect some beer pong matches & karaoke for sure.

28th November

Haldi 10AM-11AM

The haldi ceremony is usually held separately for the bride and the groom. This one is for the bride. It is a low-key event that involves the application of turmeric paste on the hands, legs and face of the bride. The groom is not allowed to see the bride after this ritual until the final wedding ceremony starts.

Wedding 6PM-12AM

This is the main event in the entire wedding when the bride and the groom tie the knot. The groom comes with the barat and is greeted by the bride side with some dhol of course as maximum number of guests would be attending this event. This is when the couple circle the sacred fire and are finally declared husband and wife.
